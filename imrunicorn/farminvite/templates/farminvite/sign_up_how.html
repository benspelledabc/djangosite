I've taken some lessons learned from the 2019 and 2020 spring invites. This time, invites are only going to people I've met personally or someone will speak on their behalf. There's a lot to say about character in a personal introduction. While I'm all for helping people become a better shooter I don't subscribe to the 'accuracy by volume' version of marksmanship. So many targets were ruined in the last invites.

"The rifle is pretty much zeroed for 100 yards so lets try 200 or 300 yards." After being several feet off at 200 yards, we checked his zero at 100 yards. He was what I later decided to call Minute of Elephant. We got him fixed up for 100 yards but then got frustrated about re-confirming at 200 instead of going strait to 500 yards. I explained why I wanted him to confirm at closer targets before pushing out to 500 yards right away. He did shoot 200 yards, once. Then went right out to 500 yards without telling me. It makes it hard to spot hits or misses when I'm already 300 yards off because you change targets without telling me first.

According to a shooter I met at a members only gun range; There are shooters that are a good enough shot with their rifle that they can cut the nuts off a gnat at 100 yards. I don't care if you're a sniper in the service or an olympic gold medalist. You're on my property as a guest and are a new to me shooter. I'll confirm your competence of shooting at 100 yards with EVERY GUN you bring. Then move out to further ranges if appropriate. IE: I'm going to frown on shooting a 9mm glock pistol beyond 50 yards. This is to make sure your scope didn't get bumped and go to Minute of Elephant. If you add Minute of Elephant and 8-11 MOA of elevation for a 500 yard shot, it goes right over the property line.

These examples boil down to full sized egos being attached and flapping in the wind. We all have egos, but it's not safe to pull them out and show everyone around when firearms are involved.

I had 28 people sign up and several back out at the last minute. It was no big deal to them because they had no commitment to the event. Meanwhile I would have already driven over an hour to get to the range. Then prep for a couple hours to get the range/field ready for said guest that never shows up. I'm now asking for some commitment to the event which is minimal but I'm thinking it'll minimize the number of no-shows/no-calls.

Shooter registration fees are easy as follows: (covers entire invite)
Men:(16y +) $50
Women:(16y +) $20
Kids:(15 and under) $5 (requires one adult registration & limited to 200 yards)
Night:(adults only) $200

I was going to do a donation system prior to being allowed to shoot my targets but I tried that in Spring of 2020 and it didn't work out at all. Of the 17 people that came to the range, there was one couple that donated which was great. Other people forgot cash, don't carry cash etc etc. That's why there is a range fee collected before the invite is even sent out. Portions of the registration may be refunded if other donations are made; Donation of steel targets, 2x4 to hold said target, brass (of a caliber I reload for), etc etc. I don't want to say it will guarantee a refund because I don't want someone showing up with 2 pieces of brass for a $10 refund request. I also don't want to refund the entire fee then have the steel targets shot and ruined because of armor piercing bullets and the list goes on. I'm sure this makes sense to everyone.

I'm rebuilding the site to get this all to work better, but for now pay the registration fee and e-mail me at: Admin@BenSpelledABC.me with your CashApp name. Mine is $BenSpelledABC. The email should also include a phone number to contact you. I prefer texting because it's a digital trail of our conversations and I hunt year round. I don't answer the phone while hunting.
